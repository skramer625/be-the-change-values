Our Values

Five values. One culture. Every decision, every day.

Kid Focus
Promises to kids are sacred. We lead with our students at the center — always.

Behaviors

  • Champion our kids, mission, and values.
  • Put students first every day, in every decision.
  • Prioritize our kids' long-term interests over short-term wins.
Radical Love
Fierce care, bold belief, and high expectations that nurture our communities' full humanity and drive transformational change.

Behaviors

  • Affirm the worth and limitless potential of every student and teammate.
  • Celebrate identity, culture, and community as sources of strength and pride.
  • Challenge inequities and behavior inconsistent with our values.
  • Act courageously, especially when it's uncomfortable.
  • Find a way or make one — radical love demands initiative and ownership.
Excellence
We seek continuous improvement in pursuit of the highest standards, with unwavering integrity to deliver outstanding results.

Behaviors

  • Hold yourself and others accountable to the urgent pursuit of results.
  • Self-reflect and seek opportunities to get better faster.
  • Develop solutions that yield strong outcomes.
TEAM
Together Everyone Achieves More. Trusting relationships and collaboration are the best way to get results for kids.

Behaviors

  • Be open-minded and assume the best.
  • Invite and honor diverse perspectives to strengthen our decisions.
  • Say the thing that needs to be said — directly and with care.
  • Give your full and frank input, then rally behind the decision.
Fun
Joyful culture, serious mission.

Behaviors

  • Take the work seriously, not ourselves.
  • Celebrate progress and wins big and small.
  • Express optimism and gratitude.
  • Bring energy, enthusiasm, humor, and passion to the work.
  • Create and contribute to engaging learning and community experiences.
Values FAQ

Real questions. Clear answers. This page grows as our community asks — and we answer together.

Does Kid Focus mean we ignore what adults — staff, families, leaders — need? ▼
Kid Focus
No — and this is an important distinction. Kid Focus doesn't mean adults don't matter; it means that when we're making decisions, the question we return to is: what serves kids best? Supporting staff well, building strong family relationships, and developing strong leaders are all in service of that goal. Kid Focus is the lens, not a hierarchy that dismisses everyone else.
How do you hold someone accountable and still lead with Radical Love? ▼
Radical Love
Radical Love and accountability aren't opposites — they're interdependent. Holding someone to a high standard is an act of belief in their potential. The behavior asks us to "affirm the worth and limitless potential of every teammate," and sometimes that means saying directly: this isn't your best work, and I know you're capable of more. What Radical Love rules out is accountability delivered with contempt, indifference, or cruelty — not accountability itself.
Does "rally behind the decision" mean I can't keep raising concerns? ▼
TEAM
Rallying behind a decision means executing with commitment — not silence. If new information emerges, or if implementation reveals a problem, you should raise it. The behavior asks you not to undermine a decision while it's being executed, but it doesn't ask you to stop thinking critically or stop advocating for kids.
Is it okay to not be a naturally "fun" person and still live this value? ▼
Fun
Absolutely. Fun isn't a personality type — it's a set of choices. The behaviors under this value are about expressing optimism and gratitude, celebrating progress, and bringing energy and enthusiasm to the work. None of those require you to be the loudest person in the room or to crack jokes in every meeting. A quiet teammate who consistently lifts others up, notices wins, and approaches hard work with genuine care is living Fun just as fully as someone more outwardly expressive.
